Rather than building a single monolithic system, what if Intuition was more like a modular protocol stack,
 with dedicated layers for different parts of human activity?

Could we imagine:

A layer for history, to track evolving ideas and reputations over time?

A layer for media, to assess information based on source credibility and social validation?

A layer for learning, where skills are endorsed, not claimed?

Or a layer for identity, where reputation is fluid and portable—not locked in one platform?

Each module could remain autonomous, but interconnect through the same semantic fabric.
Together, they might form the foundations of a new internet—not of pages, but of context. ^pVStrTzm

If we want people to adopt Intuition at scale, maybe we shouldn’t ask them to start over—but to bring their past with them, in a meaningful way.

What if there were a tool—a Migrator—that allowed users to convert their public data from Web2 (tweets, posts, reviews, issues, likes) into verifiable
 assertions inside the Intuition graph?

Could this be the bridge that lets:

Professionals prove their contributions,

Writers bring their audience and credibility,

Communities reclaim their social graph?

Maybe it’s not about abandoning the platforms we’ve used—
but about reclaiming the value we’ve created, and making it interoperable, transparent, and portable for the future.
